Serve

SciLifeLab Serve is a service under development for model and app deployment offered to life science researchers in Sweden. Currently, the service is accepting pilot users. SciLifeLab Serve allows to serve Tensorflow models, PyTorch models, Dash apps, General Python models, and R Shiny apps. The service is available to researchers in Sweden, both with and without an affiliation SciLifeLab, as well as to SciLifeLab platforms. SciLifeLab Serve, powered by STACKn, is developed and maintained by the SciLifeLab Data Centre. SciLifeLab Serve is free of charge for Swedish researchers and SciLifeLab platforms.

Why use the SciLifeLab Serve

  • Easy serving of machine learning models with versioning
  • Get FastAPI endpoints for your models
  • Allow users to use your trained machine learning models for their own predictions by adding a GUI
  • Host interactive dashboards created with R Shiny and Dash
  • Allow others to explore your data or results with with R Shiny and Dash apps
  • Opportunity to host any general Python models (get in touch with the team behind SciLifeLab Serve to discuss your particular needs)
  • Free of charge for Swedish researchers and SciLifeLab platforms

Getting an account

    SciLifeLab Serve is developed and maintained by the SciLifeLab Data Centre. At this time, the service is running in pilot mode and pilot users are accepted. The pilot projects can make full use of the resources but some interruptions may occur. Pilot projects will be used as test cases for technology development. The team behind the service is happy to discuss the particular needs of each project. In order to sign up, please fill out this form or send an email to datacentre@scilifelab.se. The team will get in touch with you shortly to discuss your particular needs.

Resource limitations

    At the moment, SciLifeLab Serve does not have any resource limitations. No specific decisions about the future resource limitations have been taken yet. When the service is launched, there are likely to be default limitations in terms of CPU and memory but we aim to cover the needs of most of the research projects.
